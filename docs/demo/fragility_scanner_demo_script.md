# Fragility Scanner Demo Script

This package turns the Fragility Scanner MVP into a founder-ready and customer-ready demo flow.

Current product truth:
- Fragility Scanner scans business text or report-like updates
- It returns a fragility score, fragility level, drivers, findings, suggested actions, suggested object focus, and scene reaction
- The current frontend shows the scanner in the existing Nexora workspace and risk view

## 1. Product Positioning

### One-line product statement

Fragility Scanner helps leaders detect where an operating system is becoming fragile before the weakness turns into failure.

### Positioning variants

- Paste an operational update and Nexora shows where the system is becoming fragile.
- Fragility Scanner turns scattered business language into an explainable risk picture.
- Nexora does not just show status. It reveals where the system may break and what to inspect next.

### Why it matters

Teams already have updates, reports, and dashboards. The problem is that weakness usually appears first in narrative form, not in a clean metric view. Fragility Scanner shortens the gap between reading an update and understanding where the operating system is thinning, what is driving that risk, and what action deserves attention first.

### How it is different from a dashboard

A dashboard shows reported state. Fragility Scanner interprets system weakness from live business language, explains the likely drivers, and visually points the team toward the fragile parts of the system. It is a decision surface, not only a reporting surface.

### Strategy lead

- Cares about: structural weakness, downstream impact, intervention priorities

- Demo emphasis: how local weakness becomes system-level risk

## 5. Demo Objection Handling

### “Is this just keyword matching?”

Answer:
- "The MVP is intentionally rule-based and explainable. That is a strength in an early product because the output is easy to audit and easy to trust. The value is not hidden magic. It is fast, readable detection of structural weakness."

### “How is this different from a dashboard?”

Answer:
- "A dashboard waits for metrics to stabilize into a report. Fragility Scanner interprets risk from the language teams are already using and shows where the system is getting weak before the full reporting cycle catches up."

### “Why not just ask ChatGPT directly?”

Answer:
- "A general chatbot can summarize text. Nexora packages the result into a consistent product surface: score, drivers, findings, actions, and scene focus. It is built for inspection and follow-up, not only conversation."

### “Can this work on real reports?”

Answer:
- "Yes, that is the point of the MVP. It is designed for real report excerpts, operational notes, and update-style input. The initial version works best when the language is concrete and operational."

### “How would a manager use this in practice?”

Answer:
- "A manager pastes a status update, sees the main fragility pattern, inspects the highlighted objects, and uses the result as a decision starting point for the next review or intervention."

### “What makes the scene useful?”

Answer:
- "The scene helps teams see what deserves inspection. It turns an abstract result into a visible system path, which is especially useful in live reviews and cross-functional conversations."

## 7. Demo Success Criteria

### Internal demo checklist

- The scanner input is visible immediately
- The example text is easy to paste and easy to understand
- The score and level are readable in under 5 seconds
- The top drivers match the scenario story
- The findings sound manager-ready
- The scene visibly reacts to the result

### Founder demo checklist

- The presenter explains the product in under 60 seconds
- The sample scenario is realistic and short
- The visual reaction is shown, not skipped
- The presenter explains why this is different from a dashboard
- The close ends with a concrete next step

### Customer meeting checklist

- The scenario sounds like the customer’s world
- The output feels credible, not theatrical
- The suggested actions feel practical
- The scene helps the customer inspect what is fragile
- The customer can imagine using it on a real update
