## 7. Output Emphasis Guide

### When supplier dependency dominates

Emphasize:
- concentration risk
- resilience weakness
- supplier exposure
- risk-zone visibility

Suggested line:
- The system is fragile because too much critical load depends on one unstable path.

### When delivery delays dominate

Emphasize:
- delivery pressure
- bottleneck risk
- service continuity
- near-term operational escalation

Suggested line:
- The main issue is not a late shipment. It is the way delay is spreading through the operating flow.

### When inventory weakness dominates

Emphasize:
- thin buffers
- short response time
- near-term exposure
- loss of resilience

Suggested line:
- The business has less room to absorb disruption, so even moderate pressure now creates outsized risk.

### When quality instability dominates

Emphasize:
- rework pressure
- throughput loss
- operational instability
- hidden cost of correction

Suggested line:
- Quality weakness is now affecting the flow of the system, not just output quality.

### When recovery weakness dominates

Emphasize:
- slow recovery
- weak fallback capacity
- repeat-event exposure
- resilience thinning

Suggested line:
- The concern is not only what happened last time. It is how exposed the system remains if pressure hits again.
