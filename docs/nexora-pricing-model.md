# Nexora Pricing and Business Model Architecture

This document defines a realistic pricing and revenue architecture for the real current Nexora MVP.

It is based on the current product truth:
- One shared core engine
- Multiple domain experiences
- Launch domains: Business, DevOps, Finance
- Core MVP flow: prompt -> scene -> risk / fragility -> executive response

This is a product business model document, not a marketing slogan and not a generic SaaS pricing page.

## 1. Business Model Summary

Nexora should monetize as a system-intelligence platform with three revenue layers:

1. Core platform subscriptions
2. Domain pack expansion
3. Premium analysis capabilities

That structure fits the architecture:
- the shared core engine is the platform subscription
- domain experiences become expansion vectors
- advanced scanning, scenario scale, and enterprise workflows become premium upgrades

Initial MVP revenue model:
- Seat-based SaaS for individuals and small teams
- Team pricing for consulting and internal strategy groups
- Enterprise pricing for cross-domain deployment, governance, and premium workflows

## 2. Primary Customer Segments

## 2.1 Consulting Firms

Why they buy:
- They need to model client systems quickly
- They need to turn complexity into executive recommendations
- They need a reusable analysis platform across multiple client contexts

Primary use cases:
- Business-system fragility analysis
- Operating model stress testing
- Scenario-based client workshops
- Executive presentation support

Why Nexora fits:
- One engine can be reused across multiple industries
- Domain packs allow firms to expand capability without retraining on a new product each time

## 2.2 Enterprise Strategy Teams

Why they buy:
- They need a structured way to analyze system fragility, tradeoffs, and interventions
- They need more than dashboards and static planning decks

Primary use cases:
- Cross-functional system mapping
- Strategic risk review
- Scenario evaluation
- Executive briefing support

Why Nexora fits:
- It connects system structure to decision consequences
- It translates model changes into readable executive insight

## 2.3 DevOps / Platform Engineering Teams

Why they buy:
- They need to understand dependency fragility, failure propagation, and resilience decisions
- They need a system-level view that sits above raw observability data

Primary use cases:
- Service dependency analysis
- Failure propagation review
- Reliability planning
- Engineering leadership briefings

Why Nexora fits:
- The DevOps domain pack turns the shared engine into a service-reliability workspace
- It can evolve later into repository and infrastructure scanning

## 2.4 Financial Analysts / Risk Teams

Why they buy:
- They need to reason about liquidity stress, exposure concentration, volatility, and capital sensitivity
- They need a narrative bridge between model signals and executive action

Primary use cases:
- Portfolio fragility review
- Liquidity and exposure analysis
- Market stress storytelling
- Risk committee preparation

Why Nexora fits:
- The Finance domain pack makes the shared engine useful for system-level financial analysis

## 2.5 Mid-Size Businesses

Why they buy:
- They need a lightweight operating-system decision tool without building a large analytics platform

Primary use cases:
- Supplier / demand / cash-pressure analysis
- Operational fragility review
- Management reporting with system context

Why Nexora fits:
- The Business domain pack is immediately understandable
- The MVP can be adopted without needing a large internal platform team

## 2.6 Independent Consultants

Why they buy:
- They need a reusable client-facing analysis environment
- They benefit from per-project repeatability and executive outputs

Primary use cases:
- Client workshops
- Strategic decision support
- Advisory deliverables

Why Nexora fits:
- The product is compact enough for solo or boutique use
- Domain packs allow expansion over time without changing tools

## 3. Core Value Proposition

Traditional tools:
- Dashboards show metrics
- Spreadsheets show models
- Static slides show conclusions

Nexora value:
- Models a live system
- Shows pressure and fragility structurally
- Makes risk propagation visible
- Supports scenario reasoning
- Produces decision-ready executive output

The product value can be expressed simply:

Nexora helps teams understand:
- what is under pressure

That value is relevant across Business, DevOps, and Finance without changing the core engine.

## 4. Pricing Architecture

The pricing model should stay simple at MVP launch and expand later.

Recommended MVP structure:
- Starter
- Professional
- Enterprise

## 4.1 Starter

Target customer:
- Independent consultants
- Small strategy teams
- Small operating teams
- Early technical evaluators

Price:
- $49 per user / month

What it includes:
- Shared Nexora core engine access
- 1 launch domain included
- Prompt-driven system analysis
- Starter demos and workspace flow
- Core risk / fragility / executive outputs
- Standard support

Recommended limits:
- 1 user
- 1 active domain pack included
- Limited number of saved projects
- Limited scenario history / replay depth

Why this tier exists:
- Low-friction entry for evaluation and early adoption

## 4.2 Professional

Target customer:
- Consulting teams
- Strategy groups
- DevOps leads
- Financial analysis teams

Price:
- $149 per user / month

What it includes:
- Shared Nexora core engine
- Up to 3 launch domains
- Full MVP prompt -> scene -> risk -> executive workflow
- Cross-domain usage within the same account
- More project capacity
- Team collaboration support at MVP level
- Priority support

Recommended limits:
- 3 to 10 users
- 3 included domain packs
- Higher scenario / replay / project limits

Why this tier exists:
- This is the main working tier for real MVP use

## 4.3 Enterprise

Target customer:
- Larger organizations
- Consulting firms with multiple teams
- Enterprise strategy functions
- Platform / reliability orgs
- Risk and finance teams

Price:
- Custom pricing

What it includes:
- Shared core engine across teams
- All launch domain packs
- Central admin / governance positioning
- Higher workspace and project scale
- Premium support and onboarding
- Access to premium scanner and scenario capabilities as they mature

Why this tier exists:
- Nexora’s architecture is strongest when multiple groups use one platform with multiple domain experiences

## 5. Domain Pack Economy

Domain packs should be a clear part of revenue architecture.

Why:
- They align directly to the product architecture
- They create horizontal expansion without forking the core
- They let Nexora grow account value as customers adopt more use cases

## Recommended MVP domain-pack model

Starter:
- Includes 1 domain pack
- Additional domain pack: $25 to $40 per user / month

Professional:
- Includes Business, DevOps, and Finance
- Future domain packs can be paid add-ons

Enterprise:
- Bundle launch domains by default
- Price future specialized packs into enterprise expansion or add-on licenses

## Domain pack monetization logic

Launch packs:
- Business
- DevOps
- Finance

Future monetizable packs:
- Strategy
- HR
- Logistics
- Energy
- Infrastructure
- Policy / Government

This supports horizontal growth:
- same customer
- more teams
- more domains
- higher contract value

## 6. Scanner Monetization

The multi-source scanner should be positioned as a premium capability, not a default MVP promise.

Why:
- It is strategically valuable
- It has higher compute / workflow value
- It aligns with enterprise and consulting needs

## Recommended scanner pricing model

Starter:
- No advanced scanner connectors
- Manual or prompt-driven analysis only

Professional:
- Limited scanner capability as it matures
- Example: document-text or structured-summary ingestion

Enterprise:
- Full premium scanner positioning
- Multi-source ingestion
- Higher-volume usage
- Connector-based deployment options

Two viable monetization models:

1. Enterprise-only feature
- Best if scanner maturity is still limited
- Keeps pricing simple early

2. Usage-based premium add-on
- Best once scanner workflows become real and repeatable
- Price by source volume, ingestion runs, or analysis credits

MVP recommendation:
- Keep scanner monetization enterprise-first
- Avoid complex usage billing at launch

## 7. Consulting Mode

Consulting should be treated as a real revenue path, not a side case.

Why:
- Consultants can become early multipliers
- They use Nexora repeatedly across clients
- They help validate the cross-domain platform model

## Recommended consulting commercial model

Option A: Consultant seat license
- Best for boutique and mid-size firms
- Example: Professional tier with multi-project usage

Option B: Consulting team plan
- Best for firms with several consultants using Nexora
- Team bundle with shared workspace/project capacity

Option C: Enterprise consulting license
- Best for larger advisory firms
- Annual contract with multiple seats and multiple domain packs

Optional later model:
- Per-client project bundles

MVP recommendation:
- Start with seat-based consulting pricing
- Do not launch with per-project billing complexity

## 8. Platform Ecosystem Potential

Nexora can grow into a platform ecosystem over time.

Future ecosystem paths:
- Third-party domain packs
- Industry-specific templates
- Developer extensions
- Partner-led vertical modules

Potential future revenue models:
- Marketplace revenue share
- Pack licensing
- Certified partner programs

MVP recommendation:
- Do not monetize ecosystem complexity yet
- Mention it only as platform upside

## 11. Revenue Expansion Strategy

Nexora should grow revenue in four directions:

1. More seats
- Individual user to team
- Team to enterprise

2. More domain packs
- Add Strategy, HR, Logistics, Energy, Infrastructure, Policy

3. More premium workflows
- Scanner capabilities
- Larger scenario capacity
- Richer replay / reporting

4. Deeper organizational adoption
- More teams using the same shared platform
- More executive workflows built on the same engine

This is strong because it matches the product architecture:
- one core platform
- multiple monetizable domain experiences
- premium capability expansion

## 12. Pricing Design Principles

To stay aligned with the real product, Nexora pricing should follow these rules:

- Keep the core engine bundled into every paid plan
- Monetize breadth through domain packs
- Monetize depth through premium analysis and scanner capabilities
- Keep MVP pricing simple
- Avoid complex usage billing too early
- Preserve a clear upgrade path from individual use to enterprise platform deployment

## 13. Recommended Initial Go-To-Market Position

For MVP launch, Nexora should be sold as:
- a cross-domain system-intelligence workspace
- for strategy, operations, reliability, and financial fragility analysis

Primary commercial wedge:
- Consulting firms
- Enterprise strategy teams
- DevOps / platform leaders

Secondary wedge:
- Financial analysis and risk teams
- Mid-size business decision teams

## 14. Final Recommendation

Recommended business model:
- SaaS subscription
- Seat-based at MVP launch
- Domain-pack expansion model
- Enterprise premium path for scanner and larger-scale workflows

Recommended launch pricing:
- Starter: $49 per user / month
- Professional: $149 per user / month

Recommended strategic logic:
- The shared core engine is the product foundation
- Domain packs are the expansion engine
- Premium scanner and enterprise workflows are the monetization upside

This model fits the real current Nexora MVP and leaves room for future platform growth without pretending the product is already a fully mature enterprise suite.
