# Nexora Fragility Scanner Revenue Engine

This document defines the revenue engine for Product A: Fragility Scanner.

It is designed for the real current product stage:
- Fragility Scanner is an MVP
- the product already demonstrates clear value through fragility score, drivers, findings, actions, object focus, and scene reaction
- the business model must be simple enough to sell early
- the pricing model must support founder-led selling, early customer adoption, and expansion into the broader Nexora platform

This is a practical monetization document, not a theoretical SaaS framework.

## 1. Revenue Objective

### Core objective

Turn Fragility Scanner into a paid SaaS wedge that can:
- win early individual and small-team users quickly
- prove repeated usage around real operational updates
- create a clear upgrade path into team workspaces and the broader Nexora platform

### Best revenue model for MVP

Recommended model:
- SaaS subscription with usage bands
- light per-seat logic for team plans
- optional add-on usage packs
- a separate consultant plan

Why this is the best MVP model:
- pure usage pricing is too unpredictable for an early SaaS wedge
- pure seat-based pricing hides the real cost driver, which is scans and analysis volume
- heavy enterprise pricing is too early for Product A
- a hybrid subscription model keeps buying simple while still giving Nexora room to monetize growth

### Target price range

Recommended early-stage range:
- free to $39/month entry level
- $99 to $149/month core professional tier
- $349 to $599/month team tier
- $249 to $399/month consultant tier for solo or boutique advisory use

### Ideal first customer profile

The ideal first paying customer is:
- a manager or consultant who already reads frequent operational updates
- feels pain from fragmented reporting and unclear risk interpretation
- can use the product without a long integration cycle
- values speed to insight more than perfect system integration

Best initial fit:
- operations or supply chain managers in mid-size businesses
- independent consultants or boutique firms doing operational diagnostics

## 2. Target Customer Segments

Segments are ranked by early adoption potential, not by total market size.

### 1. Strategy Consultant

- Role: external advisor, boutique consultant, operating model consultant
- Pain point: needs a fast way to turn messy client updates into a credible risk story
- Why fragility detection matters: it helps structure workshops, client reviews, and recommendations
- Willingness to pay: high for solo buyers because the product supports billable work
- Buying trigger: one live client use case where Nexora saves time and improves the client-facing story

### 2. Operations Manager

- Role: operations leader, plant manager, fulfillment manager, network operations lead
- Pain point: reads many updates but still struggles to see where fragility is building
- Why fragility detection matters: it helps prioritize intervention before service performance breaks
- Willingness to pay: medium to high if the product shortens review and escalation time
- Buying trigger: repeated delivery pressure, supplier issues, or buffer erosion

### 3. Supply Chain Manager

- Role: supply chain planning lead, logistics lead, procurement operations manager
- Pain point: supplier, inventory, and delivery issues are visible but not easy to connect
- Why fragility detection matters: it surfaces concentration risk, weak buffers, and unstable recovery paths
- Willingness to pay: medium to high when reliability pressure is active
- Buying trigger: recurring supplier delays, congestion, or inventory stress

### 4. Risk Manager

- Role: operational risk manager, resilience lead, business continuity lead
- Pain point: risk signals exist, but narrative updates are hard to translate into a clear exposure view
- Why fragility detection matters: it creates an explainable fragility surface from messy language
- Willingness to pay: medium, often stronger inside a team or business-unit purchase
- Buying trigger: recent disruptions, resilience reviews, or board-level risk reporting needs

### 5. Transformation Lead

- Role: transformation office lead, operating model lead, change leader
- Pain point: transformation creates instability, but weakness is hard to explain across teams
- Why fragility detection matters: it helps identify where the operating system is thinning during change
- Willingness to pay: medium
- Buying trigger: major operating change, network redesign, or transformation program review

### 6. Startup Founder

- Role: founder, COO, operator-founder
- Pain point: scattered operational signals and little time to interpret them
- Why fragility detection matters: it helps connect updates into a system-level view quickly
- Willingness to pay: medium for the right founder, but usually less predictable than consultants or managers
- Buying trigger: scaling pressure, delivery failures, or supplier dependence in a growing business

Fragility Scanner turns messy status language into a structured risk picture with score, drivers, findings, and suggested actions.

Value:

Pain:

Value:

## 4. Pricing Model

The pricing model should stay simple at MVP stage. The product needs clear packaging, low-friction adoption, and obvious upgrade reasons.

### Free

- Price: $0
- Scan limit: 5 scans per month
- Intended user: curious evaluators, warm demo leads, early self-serve users
- Features:
  - basic fragility score
  - fragility level
  - top 2 drivers
  - one example scenario library
  - limited result history

Why it exists:
- it demonstrates value quickly without creating heavy infrastructure cost
- it gets the user to the first scan and first visual reaction

### Starter

- Price: $39/month
- Scan limit: 40 scans per month
- Intended user: solo managers, individual operators, founders
- Features:
  - full fragility score and level
  - full driver list
  - findings
  - suggested actions
  - suggested objects
  - scene reaction
  - 30-day result history

Why this tier exists:
- it is low enough for founder-led adoption
- it captures individual value without requiring team rollout

### Professional

- Price: $119/month
- Scan limit: 250 scans per month
- Intended user: power users, consultants, heavier operational reviewers
- Features:
  - everything in Starter
  - longer result history
  - export summary
  - saved scenario library
  - richer comparison workflow
  - priority support

Why this tier exists:
- this is the likely main revenue tier for serious individual use
- it supports repeated usage and stronger work-product creation

### Team / Business

- Price: $399/month for up to 5 users
- Scan limit: 1,500 scans per month
- Intended user: operations teams, supply chain teams, risk teams, boutique consulting teams
- Features:
  - everything in Professional
  - shared workspace
  - shared scan history
  - team scenario library
  - team collaboration and review usage
  - admin basics
  - business support

Why this tier exists:
- it creates a simple team buying motion
- it starts the transition from individual usage to organizational adoption

## 6. Free Plan Strategy

### Free plan design

- scans allowed: 5 per month
- history: last 3 results only
- analysis limits:
  - show score and level
  - show only partial findings
  - lock full recommended actions after first successful scan

### Why this works

- the user can reach the product’s value quickly
- the plan remains cheap to serve
- the upgrade path appears naturally after one or two useful scans

### Upgrade triggers

- monthly scan limit reached
- recommended actions locked
- result history limit reached
- export unavailable
- collaboration unavailable

## 8. Customer Conversion Funnel

### Stage 1. Discovery

- User action: sees a live demo, shared result, founder outreach, or consultant referral
- System response: strong landing message and one clear sample scan
- Conversion trigger: user understands the product in under 60 seconds

### Stage 2. First scan

- User action: pastes a real or sample operational update
- System response: score, drivers, findings, actions, scene reaction
- Conversion trigger: result feels credible and easy to understand

### Stage 3. Aha moment

- User action: sees a hidden or underappreciated fragility driver become visible
- System response: scanner highlights the weak system path and gives a concrete takeaway
- Conversion trigger: user thinks, "This is more useful than reading the update manually."

### Stage 4. Repeated usage

- User action: scans more updates, tests another scenario, or shares output internally
- System response: saved history, repeated consistent outputs, growing product trust
- Conversion trigger: the scanner becomes part of a recurring review workflow

### Stage 5. Upgrade

- User action: hits scan limit, wants history, wants exports, or shares with others
- System response: clear plan comparison and upgrade prompt
- Conversion trigger: the product is now tied to real work, not only demo value

## 9. Aha Moment Design

The aha moment is:

The user pastes a normal operational update, Nexora surfaces the real fragility driver, highlights the weak system path, and the user immediately understands what deserves attention next.

Why it matters:
- this is the moment when the product stops feeling like text analysis and starts feeling like decision infrastructure

How to guide users there quickly:
- use a strong default scenario
- keep the first input easy
- put the score and top drivers first
- show the scene reaction clearly
- add an interpretation hint such as:
  - `Read the score first, then review the top drivers and highlighted objects.`
